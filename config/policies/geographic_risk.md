# Geographic Risk Assessment

## FATF High-Risk Jurisdictions
Transfers to or from FATF-listed high-risk jurisdictions require Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD). Current high-risk country codes include: IR (Iran), KP (North Korea), MM (Myanmar), PK (Pakistan), SY (Syria), YE (Yemen), SS (South Sudan), NG (Nigeria — heightened monitoring).

Any transaction to or from these jurisdictions must be:
1. Automatically flagged for compliance review
2. Escalated if the amount exceeds $1,000
3. Reported via SAR if the transaction appears suspicious

## Sanctions Screening
All wire transfers must be screened against OFAC SDN (Specially Designated Nationals) list. Transactions matching or closely matching sanctioned entities must be blocked immediately and reported to the compliance officer within 24 hours.

## High-Risk Country Monitoring
Transactions involving the following country codes trigger enhanced monitoring even if not on the FATF list: RU (Russia), BY (Belarus), VE (Venezuela), CU (Cuba), MM (Myanmar), SD (Sudan). Transfers above $5,000 to these jurisdictions require supervisor approval.

## Geographic Anomaly Detection
A transaction originating from a country not in the customer's profile history is flagged as a geographic anomaly. If the customer has never transacted internationally and a foreign transfer appears, the risk score is temporarily elevated and the transaction is held for 24 hours pending verification.
