# Customer Communication Policy

## Fraud Alert Notification Timeline
Fraud alerts must be communicated to affected customers within 24 hours of detection. Delay beyond 24 hours requires documented justification from a compliance supervisor.

## Communication Restrictions
Customer communications regarding flagged transactions must NOT disclose:
- Internal investigation details or status
- Names of compliance policies or regulatory reports being filed
- Whether a SAR has been or will be filed (tipping off is a federal crime)
- Names of other accounts or customers involved in the investigation
- The specific detection method or rule that triggered the flag

## Required Tone and Content
All customer communications must be:
- Professional and reassuring in tone
- Written in plain language (no legal jargon)
- Focused on customer action required, not internal compliance activity
- Reviewed by a compliance officer before sending if the case involves escalation

## Approved Communication Channels
- Secure in-app messaging (preferred)
- Email to verified address on file
- Phone call with identity verification completed first
- Physical mail for customers without digital access

## Template Requirements
Draft communications must include:
1. A clear subject line that does not mention fraud or investigation
2. A brief, neutral description of the account activity in question
3. A specific action requested from the customer (e.g., verify transaction, call support)
4. Contact information for the customer support team
5. A reference number tied to the internal case ID (not the SAR number)

## Review Before Send
All customer communications generated by automated systems must be marked as "requires_review: true" and approved by a human compliance officer before being sent. No automated system may send fraud-related communications without human approval.
